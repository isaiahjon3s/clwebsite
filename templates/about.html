About

CantiLever Solutions

A specialized PCB design firm focused on the needs of scientists, researchers, and instrument engineers.

Our Background

Built for scientific instrumentation

CantiLever Solutions was founded to serve a specific gap in the PCB design market: organizations building or servicing scientific instruments who need more than a generalist layout shop, but don't have the resources to maintain a full in-house electronics team.

Much of our work centers on LED illumination electronics — designing drop-in driver boards that replace aging halogen illuminators in microscopes from Zeiss, Nikon, Olympus, Leica, and others. We also design capacitive transducer front ends, MEMS and test probe interfaces, detector electronics, and the full range of control boards that make precision instruments work.

The name comes from the cantilever — the probe at the heart of atomic force microscopy, and a structure that represents precision, sensitivity, and careful engineering. We apply those same principles to every board we design.

We don't just deliver files

We're available for prototype bring-up, debug support, and design iteration as your hardware evolves.

How We Approach Design

Principled, documented, reproducible

Signal Integrity First

We start layout with the critical signal paths — high-speed buses, sensitive analog nets, clock distribution — before placing anything else. Impedance control and return path analysis are not afterthoughts.

EMC by Design

Proper stackup, guard routing, ferrite placement, and decoupling strategy are designed in from the start. Fewer board spins, fewer surprises in the EMC chamber.

DFM Discipline

Designs that can't be built reliably aren't good designs. DFM rules are applied throughout layout — not just at the end — resulting in better yields and lower assembly cost.

Tools & Standards

What we use

We work in the tools our clients use and adhere to industry standards throughout.

  • Design Tools Altium Designer, KiCad. Handoffs accepted from OrCAD, Eagle, and others.
  • Simulation LTspice for LED driver, analog, and power circuit simulation. HyperLynx for SI/PI on high-speed designs.
  • Standards IPC-2221, IPC-7351, IPC-A-610. CE and FCC pre-compliance experience for laboratory instruments.
  • Instrument Platforms Design experience with Zeiss, Nikon, Olympus, Leica, Leitz, Wild Heerbrugg, Bausch & Lomb, American Optical, Diagnostic Instruments, and Haag-Streit systems.
  • Version Control Design files managed in Git. Full revision history and change documentation included with every project.
