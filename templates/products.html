Technical Scope

Design capabilities

We work across a broad range of technologies and are comfortable with demanding physical and electrical constraints.

  • LED Driver Design Constant-current regulation, PWM dimming, over-temperature protection, and soft-start for high-power LED illumination. Experience with single-die, multi-die, and ring-light configurations.
  • Low-Noise Analog Transimpedance amplifiers, synchronous demodulation, and precision references for capacitive transducer and photodetector front ends.
  • Mixed-Signal Layout Careful partitioning of analog and digital domains, guard routing, and shielding for quiet measurement electronics.
  • Thermal Management Thermal simulation and copper pour strategy essential for LED driver boards — preventing lumen depreciation and extending service life.
  • EMC EMC-conscious layout for laboratory instruments. Pre-compliance experience toward CE and FCC marks.
Instrument Brand Experience

We have worked with or designed to the specifications of instruments from: Zeiss, Nikon, Olympus, Leica, Leitz Wetzlar, Wild Heerbrugg, Bausch & Lomb, American Optical, Diagnostic Instruments, Haag-Streit, and others. If your platform isn't listed, get in touch — we're happy to review the specs.

Examples

What these projects look like

To give a sense of scope — these are representative examples of the types of projects we take on. Every engagement is different, and we're happy to discuss your specific situation.

LED Illumination

Halogen-to-LED retrofit

A customer with an aging halogen illuminator needs a drop-in LED driver board that fits the original housing, maintains the same connector pinout, and adds PWM dimming. We design from scratch or adapt an existing topology to the mechanical and electrical constraints of the instrument.

Signal Conditioning

Detector interface board

A research lab needs a compact interface board for a photodetector or PMT — transimpedance amplifier, filtering, and a clean analog output. We handle the full schematic and layout, with attention to noise, shielding, and the PCB's physical footprint inside the instrument.

Instrument Electronics

Custom controller for a precision stage

An instrument builder needs a multi-axis controller board with encoder inputs, DAC outputs, and a USB interface — designed to fit inside a specific enclosure. We scope the requirements, select components, and deliver production-ready Gerbers.
